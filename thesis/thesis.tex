\author{Joakim Johanson Lindquister}

\chapter*{Abstract}

Using \gls{cots} software over networks that are \gls{dil} may not perform
satisfactory, or can even break down entirely. Such networks are characterized
by seeing frequent disruptions for both shorter and longer periods, as well as
high delays, low data rates and high packet error rates. In this thesis, we
design and implement a prototype proxy to improve the performance of Web
services in DIL environments. The main idea of our design is to deploy a pair of
proxies to facilitate HTTP communication between Web service applications. As an
optimization technique, we evaluate the usage of alternative transport protocols
to carry information across these types of networks.

By introducing a proxy pair, we were able to break the end-to-end dependency
between two applications communicating in a DIL network, and thus achieve higher
reliability. The proxy was designed to support the \gls{http}, the \gls{amqp}
and the \gls{coap} for \textit{inter-proxy} communication. Evaluations show that
in most cases using HTTP/TCP yielded the lowest \gls{rtt}. However, with small
message payloads and in networks with low data rates, \gls{coap} has a lower RTT
and network footprint than HTTP/TCP.
